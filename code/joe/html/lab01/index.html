Alan Turing

Alan Turing, born on June 23rd, 1923, was most famous for his contributions to computer science. In fact, he is widely regarded as the father of theoretical computer science. He was born in Madia Vale in London, and went to King's College in Cambridge at 1931. During WWII, he worked on Cryptanalysis for the UK, inventing the bombe, a way to decrypt Enigma code.
